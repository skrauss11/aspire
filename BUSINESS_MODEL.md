# BUSINESS_MODEL.md

## Business Model Thesis

Aspire Rate can monetize by helping users, advisors, and financial content platforms measure the gap between desired futures and financial trajectories.

The business should sell clarity, scenario depth, data, and workflow. It should not need to sell investment products.

## Potential Revenue Lines

### 1. Consumer Freemium

Free:

- Basic Aspire Rate calculator
- Limited scenarios
- CPI comparison
- Shareable output

Paid:

- Saved scenarios
- Advanced category assumptions
- Sensitivity analysis
- Household planning
- Exportable reports
- Data-backed defaults

Why it fits:

- Keeps the core concept accessible
- Lets power users pay for depth

Risk:

- Consumer financial tools can have high churn unless tied to repeated planning moments.

### 2. Advisor Tools

Offer advisors a client-facing planning lens.

Paid features:

- White-labeled reports
- Client scenario exports
- Meeting prep worksheets
- Assumption libraries
- Compliance review mode

Why it fits:

- Advisors need differentiated conversations.
- Aspire Rate can help explain why generic inflation assumptions miss client goals.

Risk:

- Requires careful compliance workflows and advisor-specific positioning.

### 3. Content and Newsletter

Build an editorial audience around personal inflation.

Revenue:

- Sponsorships
- Premium newsletter
- Research reports
- Affiliate partnerships only if compliant and clearly disclosed

Why it fits:

- The idea is inherently content-friendly.
- Charts and comparisons can travel.

Risk:

- Sponsorships and affiliate models can create trust or compliance issues.

### 4. Data and Reports

Sell category-specific Aspire Rate reports.

Examples:

- Homebuyer Aspire Rate by metro
- College Aspire Rate by school type
- Healthcare Aspire Rate by age band
- Family formation cost index

Why it fits:

- Data can become proprietary over time.
- Reports can support both consumers and professionals.

Risk:

- Requires strong methodology and source discipline.

### 5. API or Embedded Widgets

License calculators or data endpoints to publishers, advisors, or financial platforms.

Why it fits:

- The concept can become a layer inside other products.

Risk:

- Requires reliable infrastructure and tight output controls.

## Pricing Hypotheses

Initial hypotheses to test:

- Consumer Pro: $8 to $15 per month
- Annual consumer plan: $79 to $149 per year
- Advisor solo: $49 to $149 per month
- Advisor team: custom pricing
- Reports: $19 to $99 each
- API/widget: usage-based or annual license

These are hypotheses, not final pricing.

## Growth Loops

### Calculator to Share

User generates Aspire Gap and shares a clean chart.

### Content to Calculator

Essay explains a category, then routes to a calculator preset.

### Advisor to Client

Advisor uses Aspire Rate in a client meeting; client shares or revisits scenario.

### Data to Press

Publish metro or goal-specific Aspire Rate reports that journalists can cite.

## Monetization Principles

- Do not sell user anxiety.
- Do not monetize by pushing products.
- Do not hide assumptions behind paid walls when they affect trust.
- Do not create incentives to recommend specific investments.
- Paid value should come from saved work, richer scenarios, better data, and clearer reporting.
