## Content Mission

Make "the inflation rate of the life you want" a durable financial idea.

Content should teach people to see inflation as personal, goal-based, and future-oriented.

## Editorial Thesis

The headline inflation number is useful but incomplete. The life people are trying to build has its own basket, weights, timelines, and risks.

Aspire Rate content should show this with clean data, sharp framing, and practical scenarios.

## Voice

Comps from `design.md`:

- Morgan Housel
- Matt Levine
- Nassim Taleb

Voice traits:

- Smart
- Brief when possible
- Wry but not smug
- Specific
- Data-aware
- Calm under uncertainty

Avoid:

- Generic personal finance advice
- Hype
- Panic
- Market predictions
- "One weird trick" framing
- Motivational filler

## Content Pillars

### 1. CPI Is Not Your Life

Explain where official inflation helps and where it does not.

Example topics:

- Why your basket differs from CPI
- Why housing dominates some futures
- Why education inflation feels different from grocery inflation

### 2. The Cost of Tomorrow

Show how goals inflate over time.

Example topics:

- The home down payment target that moves
- The college fund that keeps needing recalculation
- Healthcare as a future cost curve

### 3. The Aspire Gap

Make the gap between money growth and future cost growth intuitive.

Example topics:

- Why earning more can still feel like falling behind
- The difference between income growth and asset growth
- When a positive gap changes behavior

### 4. Assumptions Matter

Teach users to inspect the model.

Example topics:

- The danger of one-number planning
- Why ranges beat false precision
- How to think about expected returns without pretending they are promises

### 5. Optionality

Frame money as the ability to preserve future choices.

Example topics:

- The inflation rate of flexibility
- The cost of staying in a high-opportunity city
- How family support changes the future basket

## Formats

### Short Social

- One chart
- One idea
- One clean punchline
- No unsupported investment claim

### Newsletter

- 800 to 1,500 words
- One central data question
- Clear chart or table
- Link to calculator or scenario

### Calculator Presets

- "Homebuyer in Austin"
- "Private college target"
- "Healthcare-heavy retirement"
- "Family optionality basket"

### Reports

- More formal methodology
- Source citations
- Data tables
- Clear limitations

## Recurring Series

- Your Life vs CPI
- The Moving Target
- Aspire Gap of the Week
- What This Goal Really Inflates At
- Assumption Audit

## Editorial Rules

- Timestamp every current data point.
- Cite primary sources where possible.
- Keep claims narrow.
- Do not imply investment advice.
- Make the chart do real work.
- Prefer examples over abstractions.
