# ICP.md

## Ideal Customer Profile

Aspire Rate is for financially aware people who feel that conventional inflation numbers do not match the life they are trying to build.

## Primary ICP

### Name

The upwardly mobile planner.

### Profile

- Age: 28 to 50
- Income: middle-high to high income, often rising
- Education: college educated or self-directed financially literate
- Location: urban, suburban, or high-cost regions
- Life stage: forming households, raising children, buying homes, planning career transitions, or building financial independence
- Behavior: reads financial content, uses spreadsheets, checks calculators, questions assumptions

### Core Anxiety

"I am doing the responsible things, but the future I want keeps moving away."

### Trigger Events

- Considering home purchase
- Having or planning children
- Tuition planning
- Major raise or job change
- Relocation
- Market volatility
- Inflation headlines
- Parent healthcare exposure
- Retirement planning moment
- Feeling behind despite income growth

## Secondary ICPs

### The Advisor-Adjacent User

Works with or is considering a financial advisor but wants better language for goals and assumptions.

Need:

- A clearer planning conversation
- A neutral framework
- A way to identify which assumptions matter

### The Financial Content Reader

Consumes newsletters, podcasts, finance X/Twitter, and long-form essays.

Need:

- A fresh mental model
- Shareable charts
- A calculator with intellectual edge

### The Household CFO

Manages family finances and wants to translate abstract goals into concrete numbers.

Need:

- Shared language for tradeoffs
- Scenario comparisons
- Family-safe explanations

## User Psychology

Users may arrive feeling:

- Competent but uneasy
- Responsible but behind
- Curious but skeptical
- Tired of generic advice
- Suspicious of official averages
- Overwhelmed by moving targets

Aspire Rate should help them feel:

- Oriented
- Respected
- Clear-eyed
- More capable
- Less alone in the ambiguity

## Objections

### "Is this just another inflation calculator?"

Answer: No. Traditional calculators apply a generic rate to a cost. Aspire Rate builds a goal-weighted rate for the user's desired future.

### "Can this tell me what to invest in?"

Answer: No. It shows the gap between the cost growth of a future and the growth assumptions for resources. Investment decisions require separate judgment and, when appropriate, qualified advice.

### "How accurate is this?"

Answer: It is scenario-based. Accuracy depends on data quality and assumptions. The product should make those assumptions visible and editable.

## Adoption Moment

The product clicks when the user sees one number that explains a feeling they already had:

> My life is inflating differently than the headline number.
