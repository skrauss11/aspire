# COMPLIANCE.md

## Compliance Posture

Aspire Rate is intended to be an educational measurement and planning tool, not a registered investment adviser, broker-dealer, tax adviser, legal adviser, or insurance adviser.

This document is not legal advice. Before launch, a qualified attorney should review the product, disclosures, marketing claims, data sources, monetization model, and user flows.

## Core Boundary

Aspire Rate may help users understand:

- How fast the cost of their desired future may be rising
- How their assumptions affect that estimate
- How their projected resource growth compares with that estimate
- Which categories drive the gap
- What questions to ask next

Aspire Rate should not tell users:

- Which securities to buy, sell, or hold
- How to allocate a portfolio
- Whether a specific investment is suitable
- Whether they can safely retire
- How to avoid taxes
- Which insurance policy to buy
- That any return, outcome, or life goal is guaranteed

## Investment Advice Risk

U.S. investment adviser analysis often focuses on whether a person or firm provides advice about securities, is in the business of doing so, and receives compensation.

Aspire Rate should avoid crossing into individualized securities advice unless the business deliberately adopts the required compliance framework.

Do not:

- Recommend specific securities, funds, managers, strategies, or allocations.
- Rank assets as best for a user's situation.
- Present model portfolios as personalized recommendations.
- Use intake answers to generate prescriptive investment actions.
- Charge for individualized securities recommendations without legal review.

Acceptable educational framing:

- "Changing the expected return assumption from 4 percent to 6 percent changes the gap by X."
- "This scenario assumes a diversified portfolio return selected by the user."
- "Consider discussing investment choices with a qualified professional."

## Marketing and Content Standards

All public claims should be:

- Truthful
- Substantiated
- Fair and balanced
- Clear about assumptions
- Clear about limitations
- Free from promissory return language

Avoid:

- Guaranteed outcomes
- Unbalanced fear claims
- Cherry-picked performance comparisons
- Unsupported testimonials
- Misleading screenshots
- Claims that imply regulatory approval

## AI Output Rules

AI agents must:

- Identify educational outputs as educational.
- Surface assumptions and uncertainty.
- Refuse or redirect requests for specific investment recommendations.
- Avoid fabricating sources, data, or citations.
- Timestamp current market or inflation data.
- Recommend professional review for financial, legal, tax, or insurance decisions.

## Data and Methodology Disclosures

Every calculator or report should disclose:

- Data sources
- Data retrieval date
- Geography
- Time period
- Whether values are user-entered, source-derived, or estimated
- Whether outputs are nominal or real
- Whether returns are pre-tax or after-tax
- Limitations of the model

## Example Disclaimer Language

Short:

> Aspire Rate is an educational planning tool. It does not provide investment, tax, legal, or insurance advice and does not recommend any security or financial product.

Long:

> Aspire Rate provides educational estimates based on user inputs, assumptions, and third-party data sources. Outputs are hypothetical, may be incomplete or inaccurate, and are not guarantees of future costs, returns, or outcomes. Aspire Rate does not provide investment, tax, legal, insurance, or accounting advice and does not recommend buying, selling, or holding any security or financial product. Consider consulting qualified professionals before making financial decisions.

## Review Checklist

Before publishing a calculator, article, report, ad, email, or AI workflow, confirm:

- No specific investment recommendation
- No guaranteed outcome language
- Assumptions are visible
- Sources are cited and timestamped
- Risks and limitations are clear
- Testimonials have disclosure review
- Sponsored or affiliate relationships are disclosed
- User data handling is consistent with privacy commitments
- Claims are backed by evidence
