# POSITIONING.md

## Category

Aspire Rate creates a category around personal future inflation.

It is adjacent to:

- Inflation calculators
- Financial planning tools
- Retirement calculators
- Cost-of-living tools
- Personal finance media
- Advisor-client planning software

But it should not be framed as a generic calculator. Its wedge is the gap between official inflation and the cost growth of a specific desired life.

## One-Line Positioning

Aspire Rate calculates the inflation rate of the life you want and shows whether your money is keeping up.

## Core Narrative

The official inflation rate is not wrong. It is just not yours.

CPI measures a broad basket of goods and services. But your future is not a broad basket. It is a set of specific goals, in specific places, over specific timelines.

Aspire Rate turns those goals into a personal inflation rate, then compares that rate with the growth rate of your money.

## Tagline

How much of tomorrow can you afford?

## Messaging Pillars

### 1. Your Future Has Its Own Inflation Rate

The cost of the life you want may be rising faster or slower than CPI.

### 2. The Gap Matters More Than the Headline

The important question is not only "What is inflation?" It is "Is my money growing faster than my future is getting expensive?"

### 3. Assumptions Should Be Visible

Users should see the costs, timelines, and growth assumptions behind the number.

### 4. Planning Is Not Prediction

Aspire Rate is a scenario lens, not a crystal ball.

## Audience Promise

Aspire Rate helps thoughtful people measure the moving target between their money and their desired future.

## Differentiation

Traditional inflation calculators:

- Start with official inflation
- Apply a generic rate
- Produce a future price

Aspire Rate:

- Starts with the user's desired future
- Builds a goal-weighted personal rate
- Compares that rate to the user's financial trajectory

## Competitive Frame

Aspire Rate should win on:

- Conceptual originality
- Clear math
- Strong editorial voice
- Scenario flexibility
- Trustworthy boundaries
- Clean, premium visual identity

It should avoid competing on:

- Stock picking
- Advisor replacement
- Budgeting minutiae
- Banking features
- Trading tools

## Tone

Use:

- Precise language
- Provable claims
- Dry wit when useful
- Respect for the reader
- Clean numbers

Avoid:

- Motivational fluff
- Panic marketing
- Alpha promises
- Crypto maximalism
- Advisor cosplay
